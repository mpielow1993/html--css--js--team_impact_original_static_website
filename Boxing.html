Boxing

What is Boxing?

Boxing, in some form or another, has existed ever since man first learned to make a fist and throw a punch. Often called ‘the sweet science,’ Boxing is a combat sport supervised by a referee and is engaged in by two competitors during a series of one to three-minute intervals called rounds. Victory is achieved if the opponent is knocked down and unable to get up before the referee counts to ten seconds (a Knockout, or KO) or if the opponent is deemed too injured to continue (a Technical Knockout, or TKO). If there is no stoppage of the fight before an agreed number of rounds, a winner is determined either by the referee's decision or by judges' scorecards.

Although fighting with fists comes naturally to people, the ancient Greeks provide us our first historical records of boxing as a formal sport; they codified a set of rules, staged tournaments with professionals, and incorporated it into the Olympics in 688 B.C. Modern Boxing is both an amateur and professional sport practiced by men and women all around the world, combining offensive punches with defensive blocks and evasive footwork. It is a fast-paced sport that produces some of the fittest athletes on the planet.

Boxing is also an intrinsic part of Mixed Martial Arts, as all MMA bouts start with each opponent standing. However, MMA Boxing has been significantly modified from traditional boxing to account for the differences in the rule sets of the two sports.

What is Boxing training like?

Training sessions typically involve some form of high intensity cardio and bodyweight workout such as:


  • Skipping rope

  • Heavy bag work

  • Speed bag work

  • Pad work

  • Footwork drills

Heavy emphasis is also placed on technique, which will be taught by our instructors and which an individual will drill with on a progressively resisting partner. With an instructor's permission, students who are deemed technically proficient and responsible enough will be allowed to attend the intermediate and advanced classes and spar.

What Boxing Classes are available?

There is no denying that getting punched is not good for your long-term health. Boxing is a tough sport that is not without its risks. Head and facial injuries are common, and many boxers carry scars from their bouts. However, learning to box doesn't necessarily mean that you have to enter boxing matches or even spar. For many of our members, boxing training rather than fighting is enough of a thrill, and they have no desire to step into a competitive ring. Boxing training is suitable for everyone and workouts can be scaled to match your experience and fitness levels.

For those who are interested in competing or are already experienced competitors, we have a solid stable of active fighters led by our Boxing coach Aaron Murray and run competition practices four times a week. Check out our table of Boxing classes available below.
